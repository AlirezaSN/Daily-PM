# Why This Topic Matters

One of the biggest sources of confusion in product organizations is the difference between:

* Product Manager (PM)
* Product Owner (PO)
* Project Manager (PjM)

Many companies use these titles incorrectly.

As a result, job descriptions often overlap and responsibilities become unclear.

Understanding the differences is critical for anyone pursuing a product career.

---

# The Simple Explanation

Imagine a company wants to build a navigation application.

### Product Manager

Decides:

> What should we build and why?

### Product Owner

Decides:

> What should the team work on next?

### Project Manager

Ensures:

> How do we deliver it on time and within constraints?

---

# Product Manager

## Primary Focus

Product Managers are responsible for maximizing product value.

They focus on:

* Customers
* Business goals
* Market opportunities
* Product strategy

---

## Main Responsibilities

### Understand Customers

* Customer interviews
* User research
* Product analytics

### Define Product Strategy

* Product vision
* Goals
* Roadmaps

### Prioritize Opportunities

* Decide what should be built
* Decide what should not be built

### Align Stakeholders

* Leadership
* Engineering
* Design
* Marketing
* Sales

### Measure Outcomes

* Adoption
* Retention
* Revenue
* Customer satisfaction

---

## Questions Product Managers Ask

* What problem are we solving?
* Why is this important?
* Which opportunity creates the most value?
* How will we measure success?

---

# Product Owner

## Primary Focus

The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing the value produced by a specific development team.

The role originates from Scrum.

---

## Main Responsibilities

### Manage the Product Backlog

* Create backlog items
* Refine backlog items
* Prioritize backlog items

### Clarify Requirements

* User stories
* Acceptance criteria

### Support Development

* Answer team questions
* Clarify scope
* Review completed work

### Accept Work

Determine whether completed work satisfies requirements.

---

## Questions Product Owners Ask

* What should the team work on next?
* Is this story ready for development?
* Does this work meet acceptance criteria?
* What is the highest-priority backlog item?

---

# Project Manager

## Primary Focus

Project Managers focus on delivering initiatives successfully.

Their responsibility is execution.

---

## Main Responsibilities

### Planning

* Timelines
* Milestones
* Dependencies

### Resource Management

* Capacity planning
* Team coordination

### Risk Management

* Identify risks
* Mitigate risks

### Communication

* Status reporting
* Progress tracking

### Delivery

* Ensure projects finish on time
* Ensure stakeholders stay informed

---

## Questions Project Managers Ask

* Are we on schedule?
* What risks exist?
* Are dependencies blocking progress?
* Will we meet the deadline?

---

# Comparing the Roles

| Area                   | Product Manager  | Product Owner       | Project Manager    |
| ---------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------- | ------------------ |
| Main Focus             | Product Success  | Team Backlog        | Project Delivery   |
| Time Horizon           | Long-Term        | Short-Term          | Project Duration   |
| Customer Research      | High             | Medium              | Low                |
| Product Vision         | Owns             | Supports            | Not Responsible    |
| Roadmap                | Owns             | Supports            | Supports           |
| Backlog Prioritization | Influences       | Owns                | Not Responsible    |
| Sprint Work            | Limited          | High                | Limited            |
| Schedule Management    | Medium           | Low                 | High               |
| Risk Management        | Medium           | Low                 | High               |
| Success Metric         | Product Outcomes | Team Delivery Value | Project Completion |

# Example: Building a Public Transportation Feature

Suppose a navigation app wants to add public transportation support.

## Product Manager

Investigates:

* Do users need it?
* Which user segments need it?
* Will it increase retention?
* What is the business impact?

Creates:

* Vision
* Strategy
* Success metrics

---

## Product Owner

Creates:

* User stories
* Acceptance criteria
* Prioritized backlog

Works daily with developers.

---

## Project Manager

Coordinates:

* Timelines
* Teams
* Risks
* Dependencies

Ensures launch occurs according to plan.

# Common Misconceptions

## Misconception 1

> Product Managers write all requirements.

Reality:

Product Managers define outcomes.

Product Owners often handle detailed requirements.

---

## Misconception 2

> Product Owners are junior Product Managers.

Reality:

They are different roles with different responsibilities.

---

## Misconception 3

> Product Managers manage projects.

Reality:

Product Managers focus on product outcomes.

Project Managers focus on execution.

---

## Misconception 4

> Product Managers tell engineers exactly what to build.

Reality:

Great PMs define problems and outcomes.

Engineering and design help determine solutions.

---

# Interview Question Example

### Question

What is the difference between a Product Manager and a Product Owner?

### Strong Answer

A Product Manager focuses on product strategy, customer needs, and business outcomes. They determine what problems should be solved and why.

A Product Owner focuses on execution within the development team. They manage the backlog, prioritize work, clarify requirements, and ensure the team builds the right functionality.

The Product Manager is generally more strategic, while the Product Owner is generally more tactical.

---

# Real-World Observation

In many SaaS companies today:

* Product Managers own strategy
* Engineering Managers own delivery
* Scrum Masters facilitate Agile processes

Dedicated Product Owner roles are becoming less common outside large enterprises.

Therefore, many Product Managers perform some Product Owner responsibilities.

---

# Key Takeaways

1. Product Managers focus on product success.
2. Product Owners focus on maximizing development team value.
3. Project Managers focus on successful delivery.
4. Product Managers answer "What and Why?"
5. Product Owners answer "What Next?"
6. Project Managers answer "How and When?"
7. Modern companies often combine these roles.
