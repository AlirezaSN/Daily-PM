# Day 1: What Is Product Management?

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should understand:

* What product management is
* Why companies need product managers
* What product managers actually do
* How product managers create value
* The difference between building features and building products

---

# 1. What Is a Product?

A product is anything that solves a problem for a customer and delivers value.

Examples:

* Banking app
* E-commerce website
* Navigation application
* Cloud storage service
* Food delivery platform
* Trading platform

People do not buy products because of features.

They buy them because products solve problems.

### Examples

| Product     | Customer Problem                |
| ----------- | ------------------------------- |
| Uber        | Need transportation             |
| Spotify     | Want convenient access to music |
| Google Maps | Need navigation                 |
| Amazon      | Want easy shopping              |
| ChatGPT     | Need information and assistance |

---

# 2. What Is Product Management?

Product Management is the discipline of identifying customer problems and delivering solutions that create value for customers and the business.

### Simple Definition

> Product Management is the process of deciding what to build, why to build it, and ensuring it creates value.

Many people think PMs manage products.

In reality:

> Product Managers manage decisions.

---

# 3. Why Do Companies Need Product Managers?

Imagine a company without a Product Manager.

* Customers want Feature A
* Sales wants Feature B
* Engineering wants Technical Improvement C
* The CEO wants Feature D

Who decides what should be built first?

Without a PM:

* Priorities conflict
* Teams lose focus
* Development effort is wasted
* Opportunities are missed

The PM helps the organization focus on the most valuable work.

---

# 4. The Core Mission of Product Management

Every PM is trying to answer three questions:

### Question 1

What problem should we solve?

### Question 2

Why is this problem important?

### Question 3

What is the best solution?

A common mistake is jumping directly to solutions.

Great PMs spend more time understanding the problem before discussing solutions.

---

# 5. The Product Management Triangle

A PM operates at the intersection of three forces:

```
      Business
         ▲
         │
         │
Customer ◄──► Technology
```

A successful product must balance:

### Customer Value

Does it solve a real problem?

### Business Value

Does it support company goals?

### Technical Feasibility

Can it be built effectively?

### Common Failures

Customer Value Only:

* Customers love it
* Business cannot sustain it

Business Value Only:

* Business wants it
* Customers do not use it

Technology Only:

* Engineers love it
* Nobody needs it

# 6. What Does a Product Manager Actually Do?

## Understand Customers

Methods include:

* Interviews
* Surveys
* User observation
* Product analytics
* Customer feedback

## Define Problems

Examples:

* Identify pain points
* Discover unmet needs
* Find opportunities

## Prioritize Work

Examples:

* Decide what gets built
* Decide what does not get built
* Allocate limited resources

## Align Stakeholders

Work closely with:

* Engineering
* Design
* Marketing
* Sales
* Leadership

## Measure Results

Questions include:

* Did the product improve?
* Did users receive value?
* Did business outcomes improve?

---

# 7. The PM Is Not the CEO of the Product

You may hear:

> "The PM is the CEO of the Product."

While popular, this statement is misleading.

A CEO has authority.

A PM typically has influence.

PMs usually do not directly manage:

* Engineers
* Designers
* Marketers

Instead, PMs align people around shared goals.

A more accurate statement is:

> Product Managers lead through influence rather than authority.

# 9. Example: Product Management in Practice

Imagine a navigation app receives the request:

> "Add voice navigation."

A weak PM asks:

> How do we build voice navigation?

A strong PM asks:

> What problem are users trying to solve?

Research reveals:

* Users cannot safely read the screen while driving
* Users miss turns while driving

Now the problem is understood.

Possible solutions:

* Voice navigation
* Larger turn indicators
* CarPlay integration
* Smart alerts

The PM focuses on the problem before choosing a solution.

---

# 10. The Product Manager Mindset

A major shift from engineering to product management is the way questions are asked.

### Engineers Often Ask

> How do we build this?

### Product Managers Ask

> Should we build this?

And before that:

> What problem are we solving?

---

# Key Takeaways

1. Products exist to solve customer problems.
2. Product Management is about making product decisions.
3. PMs balance customer value, business value, and technical feasibility.
4. PMs lead primarily through influence.
5. Great PMs focus on problems before solutions.
6. Every PM should continuously ask:

   * What problem?
   * Why now?
   * What solution?
