# Day 4: Types of Products

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should understand:

* The major types of digital products
* The differences between B2B, B2C, SaaS, Marketplace, and Platform products
* How product strategy changes based on the product type
* Why PMs should think differently depending on the business model

---

# Why Product Type Matters

Not all products are managed the same way.

The strategy for building a consumer social network is very different from managing enterprise accounting software.

A Product Manager must understand:

* Who the customer is
* How the company makes money
* How users interact with the product
* What metrics define success

Before deciding **what to build**, you need to understand **what kind of product you're building**.

---

# Major Types of Products

The most common digital product categories are:

1. B2C (Business-to-Consumer)
2. B2B (Business-to-Business)
3. SaaS (Software as a Service)
4. Marketplace
5. Platform
6. Enterprise Software
7. Internal Products

Let's examine each one.

---

# 1. B2C Products

## Definition

A B2C product is designed for individual consumers.

The company sells directly to end users.

---

## Examples

* Netflix
* Spotify
* Instagram
* TikTok
* Google Maps
* Duolingo

---

## Characteristics

* Large number of users
* Fast decision-making
* Simple onboarding
* High competition
* Strong emphasis on user experience

---

## PM Focus

* User acquisition
* User engagement
* Retention
* Growth
* Simplicity

---

## Common Metrics

* Daily Active Users (DAU)
* Monthly Active Users (MAU)
* Session length
* Retention
* Churn
* Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

---

# 2. B2B Products

## Definition

A B2B product is sold to businesses instead of individual consumers.

---

## Examples

* Salesforce
* Slack
* Jira
* SAP
* HubSpot

---

## Characteristics

* Smaller customer base
* Higher contract values
* Longer sales cycles
* Multiple decision-makers
* More complex workflows

---

## PM Focus

* Business value
* Customer retention
* Security
* Reliability
* Integrations

---

## Common Metrics

* Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
* Customer Retention
* Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
* Customer Satisfaction
* Account Expansion

---

# B2B vs B2C

| Area            | B2B        | B2C             |
| --------------- | ---------- | --------------- |
| Buyer           | Businesses | Individuals     |
| Sales Cycle     | Long       | Short           |
| Price           | High       | Low             |
| Users           | Fewer      | Millions        |
| Decision Makers | Multiple   | Individual      |
| Priority        | ROI        | User Experience |

---

# 3. SaaS Products

## Definition

Software delivered through the internet using subscriptions.

Customers pay monthly or annually.

---

## Examples

* Notion
* Figma
* Zoom
* Dropbox
* Microsoft 365

---

## Why SaaS Is Popular

Instead of buying software once:

Customers subscribe.

Benefits include:

* Continuous updates
* Cloud access
* Lower upfront cost
* Recurring revenue

---

## PM Focus

* Activation
* Retention
* Expansion
* Reducing churn

---

## SaaS Funnel

```text
Visitor
   ↓
Signup
   ↓
Activation
   ↓
Paid Customer
   ↓
Renewal
   ↓
Expansion
```

---

# 4. Marketplace Products

## Definition

A marketplace connects two or more groups of users who exchange value.

The company facilitates the transaction.

---

## Examples

* Uber
* Airbnb
* Amazon Marketplace
* Fiverr
* Upwork

---

## Two-Sided Marketplace

Example: Uber

Drivers ← Platform → Riders

Both groups must grow together.

---

## PM Challenges

* Supply and demand balance
* Pricing
* Trust and safety
* Liquidity
* Network effects

---

## Marketplace Metrics

* Number of buyers
* Number of sellers
* Successful transactions
* Take rate
* Marketplace liquidity

---

# 5. Platform Products

## Definition

A platform enables others to build products or services on top of it.

---

## Examples

* Android
* iOS
* Stripe
* AWS
* Google Maps API

---

## Platform Users

Instead of serving end users directly, platforms often serve developers or businesses.

---

## PM Focus

* APIs
* Documentation
* Developer Experience (DX)
* Reliability
* Ecosystem growth

---

## Platform Success

A platform becomes more valuable as more developers build on it.

This is another example of a network effect.

---

# 6. Enterprise Products

Enterprise software is built for large organizations.

Examples include:

* ERP systems
* Banking software
* Hospital management systems
* Supply chain management software

---

## Characteristics

* Complex permissions
* Security requirements
* Compliance
* Scalability
* Long implementation cycles

---

## PM Priorities

* Reliability
* Customization
* Performance
* Compliance
* Customer support

---

# 7. Internal Products

Not every product is sold externally.

Many companies build products for their own employees.

Examples:

* HR portals
* Internal dashboards
* Fraud detection systems
* Customer support tools

---

## Success Metrics

Instead of revenue:

* Employee productivity
* Time saved
* Error reduction
* Operational efficiency

# Common Mistakes

## Mistake #1

Applying B2C strategies to B2B products.

Enterprise customers often value reliability over flashy features.

---

## Mistake #2

Ignoring business models.

A feature that delights users but doesn't support the business may not be worth building.

---

## Mistake #3

Using the same KPIs for every product.

Marketplace, SaaS, and B2C products require different success metrics.

---

# Interview Question

### Question

How would your product strategy differ for a B2B SaaS product versus a consumer social media app?

### Strong Answer

For a B2B SaaS product, I would prioritize customer retention, reliability, security, integrations, and measurable business outcomes because customers are businesses with long-term contracts.

For a consumer social media app, I would focus on user acquisition, engagement, retention, simplicity, and network effects because growth depends on attracting and retaining a large user base.

---

# Key Takeaways

1. Different product types require different strategies.
2. B2C focuses on consumers.
3. B2B focuses on businesses.
4. SaaS depends on recurring subscriptions.
5. Marketplaces create value by connecting different user groups.
6. Platforms create value by enabling others to build.
7. Enterprise products prioritize reliability and security.
8. Internal products optimize business operations.
